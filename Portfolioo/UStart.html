U•Start Redesign








UX Designers: Two UX Designers (Including me!)


UX Researcher: Ahmed Elhussiny


Timeline: Jan 2019 - April 2019


Skills: Human Centered Design


Tools:




Intro



The Vertically Integrated projects(VIP) program at NYU Tandon was created to enable students to work on real projects and learn new skills. For my VIP I chose U•Start, which is a website where students can share ideas for new projects with others on the U•Start network in hopes of finding people with the necessary skill sets to develop the project or finding someone that has a project and wants help on it.


Problem



The U•Start site was still in development when I joined so it had various problems:

1) The information architecture and navigation was really messy. There was no official landing page.


2) The site wasn’t visually pleasing and there was information overload.




User Journeys

We started first by creating two user journeys. It's difficult to envision what an information architecture will look like without predicting what the user needs. Since we have two different types of users(Ideator and Explorer), we needed a user journey for each one. Erica served as the ideator who's looking for a developer.


Mathew served as the explorer who is looking for good projects to boost his portfolio.





Personas

We created personas for each person, including their bio, pain points, frustrations, and goals to visualize their needs.






Research



Competitive Analysis

We conducted a competitive analysis on nine different platforms and only ended up with one platform that was considered direct.




Information Archeticture

The information architecture was a little complex since we had to create a flow for the whole site based on a messy website development and what the VIP leaders wanted. Listening to what the VIP leaders wanted and envisioning what the user needed we came with the following information architecture.












Challenges

There was various setbacks that I had to overcome during the redesign of the site.


1) Teammate lead didn’t communicate effectively.


2) Had to skip on some of the wireframes and jump to hi-fi prototypes to keep up with the deadlines (very tight schedule).


3) No UX Researchers (did research on our own time).






New Design


In order to gain users, we needed a call to action therefore a pleasing and simple sign in page was important.







The "Login" button served as the CTA so it popped more than the other features on the page. We also have a sign up at the top right just in case they don't have an account.



We made sure the site was responsive as well






The page where projects are displayed served as the "landing page" which is the page users reach when they log in.





We decided to go with the dual screen on the projects page since it was proven to be the most effective. We had three different iterations with different outlines of the project feed and interviewed as many students as we could. Almost all the students chose the dual screen outline.




Once keywords are entered in the search bar, the words in the applications that match the keywords entered would be highlighted allowing the user to see the skills they match and what they lack.







The projects page was also made mobile responsive







The outline for the people page, which is the page that project owners find people on, was a little different from the projects page. We decided to showcase up to three projects next to each person’s name since from our interviews with students they emphasized that they needed to see past work as they’re scrolling to decide whether or not they want to visit the person’s profile.



Since the site doesn't specify projects' type, the user can add anything from paintings and youtube videos to design and development projects.









The profile page was the one page that was extremely packed with content and therefore needed to be designed in a way where it wouldn't cause any information overload to whoever is visiting the profile. We tried to make the content flow together as much as possible. Since we're still currently designing for U•Start, we didn't design the mobile version of the profile page yet.
